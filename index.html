What is the Challenge and the Mission?

Economic sovereignty, inequality, corruption, tribal conflicts, unemployment, poverty, environmental threats, and adaptation to an evolving demography are among the sticky problems that have plagued Ghana for years...

Fragmented knowledge—much like the parable of the blind men and the elephant—implicate progress. Especially when vital data and research is trapped behind paywalls, jargon, or isolated databases. Ghana in Data’s mission is to:

Drive Informed Civic Engagement by Making Research and Data Accessible and Approachable to Patriots Working to Build a Better Ghana.

We are a movement championing the wisdom of the collective as the missing link between Ghana’s current state and Her promising future. By combining open data and opensource, we aim to inspire an informed civic engagment.

Impact

By inspiring civic engagment through data and research, we can rewrite the rules of "politics" for the benefit of every Ghanaian.

By collecting and transforming public data into clear and immersive experiences, We can offer a more nuanced lens through which to discuss and advocate for solutions.

Here's Our Roadmap

Why Open Source?

Adopting the open source model is an invitation to the public to participate in the development of tools and resources that will help us improve key aspects of our lives as Ghanaiains.

It is an opportunity for knowledge exchange and collaboration that produce the tools that help citizens harness the power of public data.
